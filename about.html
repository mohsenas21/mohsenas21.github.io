Taylor Chan

Taylor Chan is a multi media artist who is currently completing her 3rd year of studies in the Bachelor of Craft and Design program at Sheridan College. Taylor specializes in ceramics and she is constantly looking for new ways to explore different surface techniques in her forms. She found ceramics at a young age while attending H.B Beal Secondary School in London. Taylor hopes to continue to further her education and knowledge about ceramics.

Artist Statement

I love working with clay because of its ability to represent people. Clay has natural abilities that I encourage demonstrating into my work. I love how clay slumps, cracks, breaks, warps and expands just as we all metaphorically do. My work consists of concepts that test the boundaries of maximalism. I am intrigued in how much ornamentation is too much and what the right amount is. I create functional ware that can be used in everyday situations- in hope to bring beauty into everyday life for the user. I am extremely interested in the way my work lives in the world and how people interact with my wares.

The surfaces on my pots are intended to match the fluidity of my forms. They are meant to harmonize with the form. To create my rich, intense surfaces, I use a variety of techniques that are layered upon each other to create an intense surface decoration. I encourage people to look at my work, and continue looking. I enjoy playing around with the idea of detail that is not noticeable at first glance. I would like people to interact with my work and every time they look at the piece, they notice a new detail on the surface.
